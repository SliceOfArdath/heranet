[...] Berg at the door. Then I went and talked to the Innkeeper. A nice boy, gave me a room free of charge, “for my services” he said it was. I don’t really like that, but what the gods(?). There was a bard, started playing after the sun went down. Everyone liked it, I think. The innkeeper’s kids came down to listen to the music. The bard played Ea nes de tiza(?). I don’t like the song, but he played it well. The innkeeper, Baron was his name, served me a plate, soup with some æne(?), I found it well.
The big room started to empty, it was time to sleep. I wished good night to the innkeeper and his heart-shell(???), and went to sleep.
